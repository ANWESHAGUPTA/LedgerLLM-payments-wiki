# Visa Compliance Process

## Summary
The Visa compliance process resolves financial disputes when one party violates Visa Core Rules but normal dispute rights don't apply. It provides an alternative resolution path outside the standard chargeback process.

## Details
The compliance process handles situations where:
- One party violated Visa Core Rules causing financial loss
- The affected party has no dispute, representment, or pre-arbitration rights under normal circumstances
- Standard chargeback processes don't cover the violation

### Common Scenarios
- Merchant bills cardholder for delayed/amended charges without consent
- Cardholder receives double refunds (credit processed before dispute resolution)
- Rule violations that don't fit standard reason codes

### Pre-Compliance Requirement
Before initiating a compliance claim, the filing party must:
- Give the opposing party a chance to resolve the issue voluntarily
- Document the attempt at resolution
- Only file if parties cannot reach agreement

### Resolution Process
If pre-compliance fails, Visa will:
- Review the case and evidence
- Arbitrate the situation
- Determine financial responsibility
- Issue binding decision

## Compliance notes
Pre-compliance is mandatory before filing. Parties must demonstrate good faith efforts to resolve disputes directly before escalating to Visa's compliance process.

## Technical reference
Compliance cases are separate from the standard dispute workflow and have different evidence requirements and timelines than regular chargebacks.
