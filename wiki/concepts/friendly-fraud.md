# Friendly Fraud

## Summary
Friendly fraud occurs when cardholders dispute legitimate transactions, either intentionally or due to confusion. Visa estimates that roughly three out of four chargebacks may be invalid claims of this type.

## Details
Friendly fraud, also called "first-party misuse," happens when:
- Cardholders don't recognize transactions on their statements
- Family members make purchases without the cardholder's knowledge
- Cardholders forget about legitimate purchases
- Cardholders intentionally dispute valid transactions to get refunds

### Impact on Merchants
Friendly fraud is particularly damaging because:
- The original transaction was legitimate
- Merchants lose both the product and payment
- Standard fraud prevention tools don't help
- Evidence requirements for representment are strict

### Prevention Strategies
- Use clear, recognizable billing descriptors
- Send transaction confirmations and receipts
- Maintain detailed transaction records
- Implement customer service processes for inquiries
- Use 3D Secure authentication for online transactions

## Compliance notes
Merchants can contest friendly fraud through representment by providing evidence that the transaction was authorized and legitimate. Success depends on having comprehensive documentation.

## Technical reference
Visa refers to friendly fraud as "first-party misuse" in official documentation. These disputes typically fall under consumer dispute reason codes (13.x series).

## Cross-network comparison
All major card networks experience friendly fraud, though specific statistics and terminology may vary. Mastercard and other networks also recognize this as a significant issue for merchants.
