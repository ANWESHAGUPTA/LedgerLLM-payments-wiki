# Chargebacks

## Summary
Chargebacks are transaction reversals initiated when cardholders dispute charges with their issuing banks. They result in merchants losing both the transaction amount and potentially the merchandise, while also incurring handling costs.

## Details
Chargebacks represent a forced reversal of payment where the card issuer returns transaction funds to the cardholder and debits the merchant's account. The process can be costly for merchants beyond just the transaction amount - they may also lose the related merchandise and incur internal processing costs.
